16.4 Indirect questions
The questions which have already been discussed (4.2-4.3) are direct ques­
tions (direkta frågor). By direct question we mean a real question that we
expect an answer to. But there are also indirect questions (indirekta frågor),
which are a kind of report of direct questions. In fact, indirect questions are
a kind of statement that does not expect an answer. Compare the following
direct and indirect questions:
Direct question Indirect question
Vem träffade hon igår? Jag undrar vem hon träffade igår.
Who did she meet yesterday? I wonder who she met yesterday.
Vad gör du? Jag kan se vad du gör.
What are you doing? I can see what you are doing.
Ä r Ulla hemma? Per vet om Ulla är hemma.
Is Ulla at home? Per knows if Ulla is at home.
Indirect questions are subordinate clauses which follow the word order of
subordinate clauses, that is, the subject must always come before the verb.
Thus they differ from direct questions, which are always main clauses and
which have a special word order - verb before subject. Usually an indirect
question functions as the object of a verb like undra ‘wonder’, fråga ‘ask’,
veta ‘know’, se ‘see’ and höra ‘hear’. When we discussed direct questions, we
made a distinction between yes/no questions and question-word questions.
This distinction is also important when describing indirect questions.
16.5 Indirect yes/no questions
Indirect yes/no questions in Swedish have a completely different form from
direct yes/no questions. They are introduced by a special conjunction, om
‘if , ‘whether’. This is the same word as is used in another type of subordi­
nate clause, the conditional clause (see 7.4). Compare the following exam­
ples:
Direct question Indirect question
Kommer du hit imorgon? Per undrar om du kommer hit imorgon.
Are you coming here tomorrow? Per wonders if you are coming here tomorrow.
Har någon sett Kerstin? Vi vill veta om någon har sett Kerstin.
Has anyone seen Kerstin? We want to know if anyone has seen Kerstin.
Regnar det? Jag vet inte om det regnar.
Is it raining? I don't know if it is raining.
The rule is:
An indirect yes/no question is introduced by om and always has
the subject before the verb.

16.6 Indirect question-word questions
An indirect question-word question is introduced by a question word (q-
word), but here the subject always comes before the verb. This means that
you can tell a direct from an indirect question-word question by the word
order. As you know, in a direct question the subject must come after the
verb.
Direct q-word question Indirect q-word question
När kom Peter hem? När Peter kom hem, vet jag inte.
When did Peter come home? I don’t know when Peter came home.
Vad hade han gjort? Vad han hade gjort, vet jag inte.
What had he done? I don’t know what he had done.
There is a complication when the question word functions as the subject in
the indirect question. In this case a word is put in after the question word;
this is the word som - the same word that is used to introduce a relative
clause. Compare the following examples:
Direct q-word question Indirect q-word question
Vem kom? Jag såg, vem som kom.
Who came? I saw who came.
Vad har hänt? Vi vill veta, vad som har hänt.
What has happened? We want to know what has happened.
Vems väska står där? Per undrar, vems väska som
Whose case is that? Står där.
Per wonders whose case that is.
In the last example the question word is part of the subject. In this case the
som in the indirect question is placed immediately after the last word in the
subject.
The som which is put in after the subject makes a clear distinction
between a direct and an indirect question when the word order alone does
not signal the difference.
Vem kom? Who came?
Vem som kom, vet jag inte I don’t know who came.
The rule is:
In indirect questions the word som is inserted after the subject
when the question word is the subject or part of the subject.

rollsinnehavaren. the star.
Som + preposition on its own can n be used as an alternative to där
when you refer to a place:
Huset som de bor i ska rivas. The house they live in is to be
pulled down.
Huset där de bor ska rivas. The house where they live is to
be pulled down.

### 16.1 The Infinitive Marker Att

#### Infinitive Marker

```text
att + infinitive
```

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| att läsa | to read |
| att skriva | to write |
| att förstå | to understand |

#### `att` Marker Vs `att` Conjunction

| Type | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| infinitive marker | Jag vill lära mig att tala svenska. | to speak |
| conjunction | Jag tror att han kommer. | that he is coming |

#### After Auxiliary Verbs

Many auxiliary verbs are followed directly by the infinitive without `att`.

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| Jag kan simma. | I can swim. |
| Hon måste gå. | She must go. |
| Vi vill stanna. | We want to stay. |

### 16.2 Att Clauses And The Infinitive As Subject

#### Infinitive As Subject

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| Att simma är roligt. | To swim is fun. |
| Att lära sig svenska tar tid. | Learning Swedish takes time. |

#### `Att` Clause As Subject

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| Att han kom för sent var tråkigt. | That he arrived late was unfortunate. |
| Att hon talar svenska är viktigt. | That she speaks Swedish is important. |

#### Placeholder `det`

Swedish often uses `det` as a formal subject and places the clause or infinitive later.

| With Initial Clause | With `det` |
|---|---|
| Att läsa är viktigt. | Det är viktigt att läsa. |
| Att han kom var bra. | Det var bra att han kom. |

### 16.3 Important Verbs Followed By An Att Clause Or The Infinitive As Object

#### Infinitive Complements

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| vilja | vilja + infinitive | Jag vill resa. |
| kunna | kunna + infinitive | Hon kan simma. |
| försöka | försöka + infinitive | Vi försöker förstå. |
| börja | börja + infinitive | De börjar arbeta. |

#### `Att` Clause Complements

| Verb | Pattern | Example |
|---|---|---|
| tro | tro att + clause | Jag tror att han kommer. |
| säga | säga att + clause | Hon säger att hon är sjuk. |
| veta | veta att + clause | Vi vet att det är sant. |
| hoppas | hoppas att + clause | De hoppas att vädret blir bättre. |

#### Learning Note

Some English verbs map naturally to an infinitive in Swedish; others introduce a full clause with `att`. Learn them as verb patterns.

### 16.4 Indirect Questions

#### Direct Vs Indirect

| Direct Question | Indirect Question |
|---|---|
| Kommer han? | Jag undrar om han kommer. |
| Var bor hon? | Jag vet inte var hon bor. |
| Vad heter du? | Hon frågar vad du heter. |

#### Word Order

An indirect question is a subordinate clause. It follows subordinate clause word order, not direct question word order.

| Type | Verb Position |
|---|---|
| direct question | question pattern |
| indirect question | subordinate clause pattern |

### 16.5 Indirect Yes/No Questions

#### Pattern

```text
main clause + om + subordinate clause
```

| Direct Question | Indirect Question | English |
|---|---|---|
| Kommer han? | Jag undrar om han kommer. | I wonder whether he is coming. |
| Är hon sjuk? | Vet du om hon är sjuk? | Do you know whether she is ill? |
| Har de tid? | Vi frågar om de har tid. | We ask whether they have time. |

#### Negation

In subordinate clauses, sentence adverbials such as `inte` usually come before the finite verb.

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| Jag vet inte om han inte kommer. | I do not know whether he is not coming. |

### 16.6 Indirect Question-Word Questions

#### Pattern

```text
main clause + question word + subordinate clause
```

| Direct Question | Indirect Question |
|---|---|
| Var bor hon? | Jag vet inte var hon bor. |
| Vad gör du? | Han frågar vad du gör. |
| När kommer tåget? | Vet du när tåget kommer? |
| Varför skrattar de? | Jag undrar varför de skrattar. |

#### Question Words

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| vem | who |
| vad | what |
| var | where |
| vart | where to |
| när | when |
| varför | why |
| hur | how |

### 16.7 Relative Clauses

#### Basic Pattern

```text
noun phrase + relative clause
```

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| mannen som bor här | the man who lives here |
| boken som jag läser | the book that I am reading |
| huset där hon bor | the house where she lives |

#### `som`

`som` is a common relative marker. It can refer to people or things.

| Role In Relative Clause | Example |
|---|---|
| subject | kvinnan som arbetar här |
| object | filmen som vi såg |

#### Word Order

The relative clause is subordinate, so it follows subordinate clause word order.

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| mannen som inte kommer | the man who is not coming |
| boken som jag inte har läst | the book that I have not read |

### 16.8 Isolated Prepositions

#### Pattern

In Swedish, a preposition can be stranded at the end of the clause.

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| Vem talar du med? | Who are you talking with? |
| mannen som jag talar med | the man I am talking with |
| stolen som hon sitter på | the chair she is sitting on |

#### Why It Happens

The noun or pronoun governed by the preposition is moved or represented earlier in the structure, but the preposition stays in its ordinary position.

| Base Idea | Question / Relative |
|---|---|
| Jag talar med honom. | Vem talar du med? |
| Hon sitter på stolen. | stolen som hon sitter på |

## 3. Summary

### 16 Subordinate Clauses And Infinitive Constructions

##### 中文总结

第 16 章处理更复杂的从句和不定式结构。要区分 infinitive marker `att` 和 conjunction `att`，并注意 indirect questions 与 relative clauses 都按 subordinate clause 规则组织。

##### 检查点

- infinitive `att` 和 conjunction `att` 是否相同？
- yes/no indirect question 用哪个词引导？
- relative clause 在句中修饰什么？

### 16.1 The Infinitive Marker Att

##### 中文总结

`att` 可作不定式标记，相当于英语 to；也可作连词，相当于 that。许多助动词后直接接 infinitive，不加 `att`。

##### 检查点

- `att läsa` 中的 `att` 是什么？
- `Jag tror att...` 中的 `att` 是什么？
- `kan/måste/vill` 后是否通常加 `att`？

### 16.2 Att Clauses And The Infinitive As Subject

##### 中文总结

不定式短语和 `att` 从句都可以作主语。瑞典语也常用形式主语 `det`，把真正的信息放到后面：`Det är viktigt att läsa`。

##### 检查点

- `Att simma är roligt` 的主语是什么？
- `det` 在这类句子中有什么作用？
- infinitive phrase 和 `att` clause 的结构差别是什么？

### 16.3 Important Verbs Followed By An Att Clause Or The Infinitive As Object

##### 中文总结

动词后接什么补语要按搭配记。`vill/kan/försöker` 常接 infinitive；`tror/säger/vet/hoppas` 常接 `att` 从句。

##### 检查点

- `Jag vill resa` 为什么没有 `att`？
- `Jag tror att...` 后面是什么结构？
- 为什么这类动词最好按 pattern 记？

### 16.4 Indirect Questions

##### 中文总结

Indirect question 是嵌入在另一个句子里的问题内容。它不是直接问句，所以词序按 subordinate clause 来处理。

##### 检查点

- `Jag undrar om han kommer` 中的问题在哪里？
- indirect question 是否使用直接问句词序？
- 哪些动词常引出 indirect question？

### 16.5 Indirect Yes/No Questions

##### 中文总结

间接 yes/no question 用 `om` 引导，相当于 whether/if。它后面是 subordinate clause，不是倒装问句。

##### 检查点

- `om` 在 indirect question 中表示什么？
- `Kommer han?` 变成 indirect question 后词序怎样？
- `inte` 在 subordinate clause 中通常放在哪里？

### 16.6 Indirect Question-Word Questions

##### 中文总结

带疑问词的 indirect question 保留疑问词，但后面用从句词序：`var hon bor`，不是 `var bor hon`。

##### 检查点

- `Var bor hon?` 嵌入后为什么变成 `var hon bor`？
- `vart` 和 `var` 的差别是否仍然保留？
- indirect question 中疑问词是否消失？

### 16.7 Relative Clauses

##### 中文总结

Relative clause 修饰前面的名词。`som` 很常见，可指人或物。由于 relative clause 是 subordinate clause，`inte` 等句子副词按从句位置排列。

##### 检查点

- relative clause 修饰什么？
- `som` 可以指人还是物？
- `som inte kommer` 中 `inte` 的位置说明了什么？

### 16.8 Isolated Prepositions

##### 中文总结

瑞典语常允许介词留在句末，尤其在疑问句和关系从句中：`Vem talar du med?`、`stolen som hon sitter på`。

##### 检查点

- `med` 在 `Vem talar du med?` 中为什么留在句末？
- `stolen som hon sitter på` 中 `på` 对应哪个名词？
- 这种结构在英语中是否也常见？
