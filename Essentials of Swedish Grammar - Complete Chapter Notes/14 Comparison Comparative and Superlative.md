14.1 General comments on comparison
Certain verbs can be used to compare different people or things:
likna, vara lik ‘resemble’, ‘be like’
Maria liknar sin mamma.
Maria is like her mother.
Maria är lik sin mamma.
påminna om ‘remind (you) o f
Peter påminner om sin pappa. Peter reminds you of his father.
(He looks a bit like his father.)
se ut som ‘look like’
En zebra ser ut som en häst i A zebra looks like a horse in
randig pyjamas. striped pyjamas.
låta som ‘sound like’
Jag låter som en hes kråka idag. I sound like a hoarse crow today
kännas som ‘feel like’
D et här tyget känns mjukt som This material feels as soft as silk.
silke.
Adjectives have special forms when you compare to what degree a certain
quality occurs:
Jan är lat. Jan is lazy.
Peter är lika lat som Jan. Peter is as lazy as Jan.
Men Eva är latare än Jan. But Eva is lazier than Jan.
Rune är latast av alla. Rune is the laziest of all.
If the quality occurs to the same degree, the words lika . . . som are used in
Swedish, and ‘as . .. as’ in English.
In both Swedish and English the adjective has special forms when a
quality occurs to a higher degree or to the highest degree. The form that
denotes the higher degree is called the comparative (komparativ) and is
formed by adding the ending are in Swedish and the ending ‘er’ in English.
When a certain quality occurs to the highest degree the term superlative
(superlativ) is used. The adjective in Swedish then has the ending ast and in
English the ending ‘est’. The adjective can thus have the following forms:

samma qualifies does not have an article or the definite form. The forms of
annan are given in 13.5.

14.2 The comparative form of the adjective
It is easy to make the comparative form of adjectives. You just add are to
the basic form:
B A S I C F O R M C O M P A R A T I V E
lat + are -> latare
lazy lazier
varm + are varmare
hot hotter
kail + are kallare
cold colder
trött + are tröttare
tired more tired
trevlig + are trevligare
nice nicer
A comparative never changes its form. Thus it has the same form with an en
noun as with an ett noun in the singular or with a noun in the plural:
Den här tröjan är varmare än This sweater is warmer than that
den där. one.
D et här huset är varmare än This house is warmer than that
det där. one.
De här långkalsongerna är These long-johns are warmer
varmare än de där. than those.
An adjective in the comparative form can be used in various ways in a
sentence:
Kajsa är trevligare än Joakim. Kajsa is nicer than Joakim.
Hon är trevligare på morgonen än She is nicer in the morning than
på kvällen. in the evening.
Du har ett trevligare jobb än jag. You have a nicer job than me.
D et är trevligare att åka tåg It is nicer to go by train than
än buss. by bus.
Vi städar tillsammans. D et är Let’s tidy up together. It’s
trevligare. nicer.

14.3 The superlative forms of the adjective
An adjective in the superlative has two forms. As well as the usual superla­
tive form which ends in ast there is a special definite form which is formed by
adding e:
BASIC FORM SUPERLATIVE SUPERLATIVE
DEFIN ITE FORM
lat + ast —► latast + e lataste
lazy laziest laziest
varm + ast varmast + e varmaste
hot hottest hottest
kail + ast kallast + e kallaste
cold coldest coldest
trött + ast tröttast -I- e tröttaste
tired most tired most tired
trevlig + ast trevligast + e trevligaste
nice nicest nicest
An adjective in the superlative can be used attributively and stand immedi­
ately in front of the noun it qualifies. In this case the definite form with e is
always used. Note that the noun must also have the definite form and that
there must be a definite article (den, det, de) in front of it (11.3).
Det här är den varmaste tröjan. This is the warmest sweater.
Det här är det kallaste rummet. This is the coldest room.
Per och Eva är de trevligaste Per and Eva are the nicest
människorna i min klass. people in my class.
If the noun is modified by a genitive or a possessive pronoun, the definite
article before the noun and the definite form disappear. But the superlative
must still have the definite form with e:
Husets trevligaste rum ligger på The nicest room in the house is
bottenvåningen. on the bottom floor.
Rune är min närmaste vän. Rune is my closest friend.
When the superlative is used predicatively, you can choose between using
the superlative form without e and the definite form with e preceded by the
definite article (den, det, de):
Den här tröjan är varmast.
This sweater is the warmest.
Den här tröjan är den varmaste.
Det här rummet är kallast i hela
lägenheten. This room is the coldest in the
Det här rummet är det kallaste i whole flat.
hela lägenheten.
Per och Eva är trevligast i min klass.
Per and Eva are the nicest (people)

in my class.
min klass.

14.6 Making the comparative and the superlative
with mer and mest
As in English, certain adjectives cannot take an ending in the comparative
and superlative. Instead they take the word mer ‘m ore’ to make the com­
parative and the word mest ‘most’ to make the superlative:
Eva är mer energisk än Peter. Eva is more energetic than Peter.
Olle är mest sympatisk av mina Olle is the most likeable of my
grannar. neighbours.
Kristina är mer cynisk än Ann. Kristina is more cynical than Ann.
This is the rule:
Adjectives that end in isk and present and past participles form
the comparative and superlative with mer and mest.
As in English, there is also a tendency to choose the forms with mer and
mest with long adjectives, which would become very heavy with the are and
ast endings:
Han är Sveriges mest framgångs­ He is Sweden’s most successful
rika affärsman. businessman.
Det vore den mest lyckosamma It would be the happiest
lösningen. solution.
Adjectives and past participles change their forms in the usual way (11.8)
when they come after mer and mest. Present participles, however, never
change their form (9.14).
Han blev mer energisk med åren. He became more energetic as the
years passed.
De blev mer energiska med åren. They became more energetic as the
years passed.
Eva blev mer och mer irriterad. Eva got more and more irritated.
Men mest irriterade var hennes But the most irritated people were
vänner. her friends.
Peter är mer förstående än Olle. Peter is more understanding
than Olle.

14.7 Adverbs. Words denoting degree, quantity and number
Many adverbs can also be compared, particularly those that denote manner
(compare 2.8). If the adverb is formed from an adjective, it usually has the
same forms as the adjective in the comparative and superlative:
Peter sjunger inte lika vackert Peter doesn’t sing as beautifully
som Eva. as Eva.
Hon sjunger vackrare. She sings more beautifully.
Men allra vackrast sjunger Rolf. But Rolf sings most beautifully of all.
Words that denote degree, quantity and number often have special forms in
the comparative and superlative. Note also the adverb gärna ‘gladly’:

### 14.2 The Comparative Form Of The Adjective

#### Regular Pattern

```text
adjective + -are
```

| Positive | Comparative | English |
|---|---|---|
| billig | billigare | cheaper |
| snabb | snabbare | faster |
| intressant | intressantare | more interesting |

#### With `än`

The comparative is often followed by `än`.

| Swedish | English |
|---|---|
| Den här boken är billigare än den där. | This book is cheaper than that one. |
| Maria är yngre än Peter. | Maria is younger than Peter. |

#### Agreement

The comparative form does not change for gender or number.

| Subject | Predicate |
|---|---|
| Bilen är snabbare. |
| Huset är snabbare. |
| Bilarna är snabbare. |

### 14.3 The Superlative Forms Of The Adjective

#### Regular Pattern

| Positive | Superlative | Definite Superlative |
|---|---|---|
| billig | billigast | billigaste |
| snabb | snabbast | snabbaste |
| ung | yngst | yngsta |

#### Use

The superlative can be used predicatively or attributively.

| Use | Swedish | English |
|---|---|---|
| predicative | Den här bilen är billigast. | This car is cheapest. |
| attributive | den billigaste bilen | the cheapest car |

#### Definite Form

When the superlative is used with a definite noun phrase, it commonly takes `-e` or `-a` in the ending `-aste`.

```text
den + superlative definite form + definite noun
```

### 14.4 Adjectives That End In Er, El Or En

#### Main Pattern

Some adjectives lose an unstressed vowel before the comparative and superlative endings.

| Positive | Comparative | Superlative |
|---|---|---|
| vacker | vackrare | vackrast |
| enkel | enklare | enklast |
| mogen | mognare | mognast |

#### Why It Matters

The comparison ending is regular, but the stem may be shortened.

```text
vacker -> vackr- + are/ast
enkel -> enkl- + are/ast
```

## 3. Summary

### 14 Comparison. Comparative And Superlative

##### 中文总结

第 14 章处理比较。规则形容词常用 `-are` 构成比较级，用 `-ast` 构成最高级；长形容词或分词式形容词常用 `mer/mest`。另外要单独记不规则形式。

##### 检查点

- `än` 在比较句中表示什么？
- `-are` 和 `-ast` 分别是什么？
- 什么时候更自然地用 `mer/mest`？

### 14.1 General Comments On Comparison

##### 中文总结

比较可以表达“相似”“同等”和“更……”。同等比较常用 `lika ... som`；比较级后表示 than 用 `än`。

##### 检查点

- `lika ... som` 对应英语什么结构？
- `än` 在句中连接什么？
- `se ut som` 和 `låta som` 分别表达什么感官？

### 14.2 The Comparative Form Of The Adjective

##### 中文总结

规则比较级常用 `-are`，并且比较级形式通常不随名词性数变化。`än` 表示“比”。

##### 检查点

- `billig` 的比较级是什么？
- 比较级是否还要加 `-t` 或 `-a`？
- `än` 后面接被比较对象。

### 14.3 The Superlative Forms Of The Adjective

##### 中文总结

规则最高级常用 `-ast`。放在 definite noun phrase 中时常出现 `-aste`：`den billigaste bilen`。

##### 检查点

- `billigast` 和 `billigaste` 的环境有什么不同？
- 最高级作表语时可否不加 definite article？
- definite noun phrase 中名词通常是什么形式？

### 14.4 Adjectives That End In Er, El Or En

##### 中文总结

以 `-er/-el/-en` 结尾的一些形容词在构成比较级和最高级时会丢掉非重读元音，如 `vacker -> vackrare -> vackrast`。

##### 检查点

- `vacker` 的比较级是什么？
- `enkel` 是否直接变成 `enkelare`？
- 这里变化的是结尾还是词干？

### 14.5 Irregular Adjectives

##### 中文总结

不规则比较级和最高级需要单独记忆。最常见的是 `bra - bättre - bäst`、`dålig - sämre - sämst`、`liten - mindre - minst`。

##### 检查点

- `bra` 的比较级和最高级是什么？
- `gammal` 的比较级为什么不是规则 `gammalare`？
- `liten` 的最高级是什么？

### 14.6 Making The Comparative And The Superlative With Mer And Mest

##### 中文总结

长形容词和许多分词式形容词常用 `mer/mest` 构成比较：`mer intressant`、`mest komplicerad`。形容词本身在需要时仍按普通形容词规则变化。

##### 检查点

- `mer` 对应 comparative 还是 superlative？
- `mest` 后的形容词是否完全不变化？
- 哪些形容词更适合用 `mer/mest`？

### 14.7 Adverbs. Words Denoting Degree, Quantity And Number

##### 中文总结

数量和程度词也有比较形式。`många` 的比较级/最高级是 `fler/flest`，用于可数名词；`mycket` 对应 `mer/mest`，用于数量或程度。

##### 检查点

- `fler` 和 `mer` 的区别是什么？
- `minst` 对应哪个 positive form？
- `flest böcker` 为什么不用 `mest böcker`？
