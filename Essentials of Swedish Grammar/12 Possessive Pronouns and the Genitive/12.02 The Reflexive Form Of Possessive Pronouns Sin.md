# 12.2 The Reflexive Form Of Possessive Pronouns: Sin

## Source Correspondence

This section explains `sin/sitt/sina`, the reflexive possessive pronoun. It is used when the possessor is the subject of the same clause.

## Basic Rule

```text
subject + verb + sin/sitt/sina + noun
```

The possessive points back to the subject.

| Swedish | Meaning | Possessor |
|---|---|---|
| Olle kysser sin fru. | Olle kisses his wife. | Olle's own wife |
| Olle kysser hans fru. | Olle kisses his wife. | another man's wife |

## Forms

| Noun Type | Form | Example |
|---|---|---|
| `en` word | sin | sin bil |
| `ett` word | sitt | sitt hus |
| plural | sina | sina barn |

## Clause Boundary

`sin/sitt/sina` belongs to the clause in which it appears. It normally refers to the subject of that clause, not to a noun in another clause.

| Sentence Type | Pattern |
|---|---|
| same clause | subject -> `sin/sitt/sina` |
| different clause | use the possessive that matches the intended possessor |

## Study Notes / Summary

### 中文总结

`sin/sitt/sina` 是反身所有代词，核心规则是“指回同一分句的主语”。`Olle kysser sin fru` 表示 Olle 亲自己的妻子；`Olle kysser hans fru` 表示亲另一个男人的妻子。
