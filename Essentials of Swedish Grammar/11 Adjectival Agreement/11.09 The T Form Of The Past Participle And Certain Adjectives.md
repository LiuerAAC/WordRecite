# 11.9 The T Form Of The Past Participle And Certain Adjectives

## Source Correspondence

This section treats spelling and form changes when certain adjectives or participles take the `-t` form.

## General Point

The `ett` form often requires `-t`, but spelling changes may occur depending on the final sound or spelling of the base.

| Base Type | T Form Issue |
|---|---|
| adjective ending in consonant | add `-t` where possible |
| participle ending in `-d` | may become `-tt` or show other adjustment |
| forms ending in difficult clusters | spelling may simplify |

## Study Notes / Summary

### 中文总结

`ett` 形式中的 `-t` 有时引发拼写变化，尤其是过去分词和某些形容词。遇到特殊形式要按词条记。
