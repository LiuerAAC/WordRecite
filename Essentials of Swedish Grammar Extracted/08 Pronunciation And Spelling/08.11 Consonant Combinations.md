# 8.11 Consonant combinations

## Extracted Text

8.11 Consonant combinations
As well as learning to pronounce single consonant sounds you will also have
to learn to pronounce various combinations of consonants. There are quite a
few of them, as Swedish, just like English, can have two or even three
consonant sounds before a vowel. When there are three consonants, the first
one is always s, as in strand ‘beach’.
Note that k and g are always pronounced before an n at the beginning of a
word:
knä knee
knacka knock
gnaga gnaw
A fter a vowel gn is pronounced ng + n:
vagn carriage
lugn calm
