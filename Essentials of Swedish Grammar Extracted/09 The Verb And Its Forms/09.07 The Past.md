# 9.7 The past

## Extracted Text

9.7 The past
The past (preteritum) has the endings de and te. The ending te is used if the
basic form ends in a voiceless consonant. Otherwise de is used (after the
vowel a which is a voiced sound and after voiced consonants). Voiceless
consonants are p, t, k and s. If the basic form ends in one of these
consonants, use te. Otherwise use de.
B A S IC FORM P A ST
öppna + de — ► öppnade opened
fråga + de frågade asked
ring + de ---- ► ringde rang
känn + de kände knew; felt
ie basic form ends in a voiceless consonant:
B A S IC FORM P A ST
köp + te ---- ► köpte bought P
byt + te bytte changed t
rök + te rökte smoked k
läs + te läste read s
The rule for forming the past is thus:
Past: basic form + d e
basic form + te after voiceless consonants (p, t, k, s)
