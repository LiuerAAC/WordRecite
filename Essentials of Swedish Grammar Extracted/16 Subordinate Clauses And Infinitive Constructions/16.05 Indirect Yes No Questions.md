# 16.5 Indirect yes/no questions

## Extracted Text

16.5 Indirect yes/no questions
Indirect yes/no questions in Swedish have a completely different form from
direct yes/no questions. They are introduced by a special conjunction, om
‘if , ‘whether’. This is the same word as is used in another type of subordi­
nate clause, the conditional clause (see 7.4). Compare the following exam­
ples:
Direct question Indirect question
Kommer du hit imorgon? Per undrar om du kommer hit imorgon.
Are you coming here tomorrow? Per wonders if you are coming here tomorrow.
Har någon sett Kerstin? Vi vill veta om någon har sett Kerstin.
Has anyone seen Kerstin? We want to know if anyone has seen Kerstin.
Regnar det? Jag vet inte om det regnar.
Is it raining? I don't know if it is raining.
The rule is:
An indirect yes/no question is introduced by om and always has
the subject before the verb.
